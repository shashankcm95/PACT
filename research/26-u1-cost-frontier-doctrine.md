# PACT U1 Doctrine — The Uniqueness Cost Frontier: An Anchor Ladder, Not a Lock

**Status**: Research note (design doctrine) · **Date**: 2026-07-05 · **Anchors to**: PACT-NORTH-STAR §4 (U1 frontier), §3 NS-2/NS-7/NS-9/NS-10, `docs/PRD.md` §8/§10 · **Revision**: hardened v2 (review-board findings folded)

## TL;DR

U1 (one-human-one-`UID`) is a **cost frontier PACT contains, not a lock it closes** (PRD §10; north-star §4). The load-bearing thesis: **every non-personhood Sybil defense reduces to raising a cost, and — for a divisible, reusable, parallelizable resource like money or compute — no protocol rule is known to make that cost grow linearly (let alone super-linearly) in the number of identities.** This is now argued in two 2026 preprints [arXiv:2601.03923; arXiv:2605.18990], cited here as `[SOURCED]` (see §1 — the containment thesis does **not** rest on them; it stands on PACT's own `rootOf` reasoning). PACT's honest ceiling: it climbs an **anchor ladder** — OAuth provenance → device attestation → stake-and-slash → personhood oracle — where each rung is a distinct world-anchored signal with a **loud honest ceiling**, and only the top rung escapes "money" at the price of a heavy external anchor PACT does not build (and even then only at the enrollment layer — §2, §6). The A.3 root attestation (Sigstore/Rekor, 2026-07-05) hardened **root-key provenance**, not uniqueness — and it is now the **6th world-anchored signal, MERGED (PR #58; `docs/PRD.md` §8 reads "Today: 6")** — a *provisional* single-log HARDEN (split-view open, Q6; same-uid file-perm custody; one run). The whole S1-S5 stake arc is SHADOW and **narrows** the issuance-cost axis; it does not harden it (NS-7/NS-9).

> This note surveys one *possible* future rung (device/hardware attestation) that is **not** a currently-decided PACT direction — see §2, observation 3. The "anchor ladder" is a **complementary lens** over the existing north-star §4 U1 frontier and the PRD §8 signal-count model — a taxonomy of cost-shapes over the same frontier, not a replacement framing.

## 1. The cost-frontier thesis (why U1 is contained, not closed)

The PACT spine has always held that the Sybil unit is the **human root** (`rootOf`, NS-4): cap *effective presence*, never nominal identities. `rootOf` structurally defeats persona-*multiplication* — a Sybil's N personas collapse to one root (`stake-anchor.js:80-83`, the `rootOf`-keyed crater-quorum Set; JSDoc at :53-57). What `rootOf` **cannot** defeat is **N distinct human roots**: a wealthy attacker who buys N genuine roots is indistinguishable from N honest humans at the identity layer (PRD §10, U1 risk row; north-star §4).

Two 2026 papers argue that a purely economic defense is a losing arms race. **Both were verified to exist and match topic** (WebFetch, 2026-07-05): [arXiv:2601.03923] = *Human Challenge Oracle* (Maleki, Sainz, Legarda, Jan 2026); [arXiv:2605.18990] = *Concave is the New Linear: The Impossibility of Anti-Plutocratic DAO Governance* (Bennett, Vander Vos, Le, Belenkiy, May 2026). They are real and on-point; their **internal theorem/lemma numbers are not independently re-derived here**, and (per NS-7) an external result *narrows*, it does not *harden*. Treat the theorem numbers as reported, not re-derived:

- **The amortization reduction** [arXiv:2601.03923, §5 — `[SOURCED]`]: any resource that is *divisible, additive-in-influence, temporally reusable, and identity-transferable* is claimed to admit **sublinear (amortizable) cost of influence concentration**. Money and compute satisfy all four ("computational resources can be reused indefinitely and parallelized"; "economic capital can often be divided or delegated across multiple identities"). Its positive result (linear Sybil cost) is claimed to hold *only* because its chosen resource — real-time human cognition — is **non-amortizable**.

- **The plutocracy impossibility** [arXiv:2605.18990, Thm 4.2/4.3/5.5, Lemma 5.1 — `[SOURCED]`]: for the money subcase, "every nontrivial voting rule is plutocratic under unrestricted Sybil wallet splitting," surviving per-wallet / splitting / voting costs ("costs only reduce the slope, they cannot eliminate linear growth"); even *concave* dampening (quadratic voting, `f(w)=sqrt(w)`) is claimed "asymptotically linear-exploitable" by splitting evenly across `n` wallets. **Applicability gap (do not paper over):** this result is stated for **voting rules over wallet-splitting**. PACT's `rootOf` model is not a wallet-split voting rule, so the reduction transfers to PACT only if PACT is *shown* to satisfy the divisible/additive/reusable/transferable preconditions for its scarce resource. That bridge is asserted here as motivation, **not established**.

Buterin's framing sits between: the *ideal* is super-linear cost (`N` identities for ~`N^2`), "only achievable with proof-of-personhood"; without it, "decentralized governance becomes much easier to capture by very wealthy actors" [vitalik.eth.limo/general/2023/07/24/biometric.html — external, unverified here].

**Doctrine consequence — resting on PACT's own reasoning, not on the preprints.** PACT does not promise to close U1. It promises to (a) collapse persona-multiplication structurally (done, `rootOf` — verified in-source), and (b) climb an anchor ladder that raises the *cost of a distinct root* — while reporting, loudly and permanently, that this only **contains** U1 (NS-9). The apex ("no self-assertion launders into legitimacy") survives an open U1 precisely because every weight is SHADOW until the frontier is contained *and* U2 closes (NS-8). This containment claim holds even if the two preprints are later revised or retracted; they are corroborating literature, not the load-bearing support.

> ⚠️ **Over-claim guard.** Do not restate "raises the cost of a root" as "makes roots unique." Do not restate "two preprints argue X" as "a theorem proves X." Any wave that treats a cost floor as a uniqueness lock, or an unverified preprint as a settled proof, has drifted (north-star §6: a guard satisfied by the cheap not the unforgeable thing; the close→over-claim reflex applied to the note's *own* epistemic status).

## 2. The anchor ladder

Each rung is a **distinct world-anchored signal** with its own cost-shape, its own narrow claim, and its own **anchor relocation** (where trust actually comes to rest). The honest-ceiling column is the NS-9 line for each.

| Rung | Cost-shape | What it proves | Honest ceiling (NS-9) | Anchor relocation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **(1) Identity anchor** — OAuth/OIDC root attestation (A.3) | ~0 marginal cost | **Provenance** of the root key: this `K_root_pub` was minted by a party who controlled a real OIDC identity, tamper-evidently logged | Does **not** prove a *distinct real human*; bottoms out in the OIDC provider = the U1 frontier itself [`sigma-root-deploy.md`] | Trust relocates to Google/OIDC + the Sigstore/Fulcio/Rekor PKI + transparency log |
| **(2) Device / hardware attestation** — FIDO/WebAuthn, App Attest, Play Integrity, Secure Enclave. **NOT a currently-decided PACT direction** (absent from north-star §4/§5 and the PRD roadmap; surveyed as a ladder POSITION) | Money-**SUNK** (buy a genuine device) + anti-VM/anti-bot floor | A **genuine device of class X ran this**, not a commodity VM | Proves genuine-**device**, **not** unique-human; consumer attestation is *engineered to be un-dedup-able*, so M devices read as M apparent humans by design — a Sybil-laundering surface if ever read as uniqueness (§5) | Trust relocates to Apple / Google / FIDO manufacturing + PKI |
| **(3) Stake + slash** — the S1-S6 arc | Money-**AT-RISK only at S6**; the SHADOW rung 3 as-built is a **zero-cost presence lock** (no amount, no `anchor_ref`: `stake.js:3-10`) | A human root posted a **forfeitable** commitment (at S6); today, only a *presence* marker | In-memory (S1-S5) is **presence, not cost** — neither at-risk nor sunk; no real forfeiture until S6 chain-settlement, which is external/unbuilt (§3, §4, §7) | Trust relocates to the chain settlement layer + the slash-attestation quorum |
| **(4) Personhood oracle** — World ID, BrightID, Idena, PoH, personhood credentials | The oracle's own enrollment cost (iris scan, social-graph, synchronous liveness) | **One credential per enrolled-biometric** via nullifier — escapes PER-IDENTITY money *at the identity layer* | **Re-admits money at the enrollment/resale layer** (verified-ID black market, rented/coerced iris, enrollment farms — §6); uniqueness is one-credential-per-enrolled-biometric, **not per-human**; inherits the oracle's trust/regulatory/coercion risks wholesale (§5, §6) | Trust relocates entirely to the external oracle (Tools for Humanity, the social graph, etc.) |

Three structural observations the table encodes:

1. **Every rung relocates the anchor** rather than eliminating it. There is no rung where "PACT itself proves uniqueness." This is not a defect to be engineered away — it is NS-10 (build the novel boundary greenfield, never over-claim reuse covers it) applied to U1. The honest artifact is the *relocation map*, not a closure claim.

2. **The rungs are not a strict hierarchy of strength; they are different axes.** Rung 2 (device) can be strong on custody/liveness while contributing *nothing* to uniqueness (§5, §6). **Reporting rung 2 as a U1 advance is the single most tempting over-claim; the doctrine forbids it.** (Preserved verbatim from the reviewed draft — this is load-bearing honesty and must not soften.)

3. **Rung 2 is a surveyed POSITION, not a PACT decision.** Device/hardware attestation appears in neither north-star §4 (the three frontiers), §5 (decided/rejected directions), nor the PRD roadmap. Adopting it would require a deliberate north-star §5 amendment. It is included so the ladder is complete, and precisely so §5/§9 can route a future Secure-Enclave key to the *custody* column and forbid reading it as a U1/axis-4 signal.

## 3. Sunk cost vs. money-at-risk — does at-risk dominate?

The doctrine spine asked whether at-risk (slashable) capital **strictly dominates** a sunk cost (a device you keep). **The economic literature says no — neither strictly dominates; they have distinct, non-comparable failure modes** [timroughgarden.github.io/fob21/reports/r3.pdf; arXiv:2401.05797 — both external, unverified here].

**The case for at-risk (rung 3 over rung 2):**

- At-risk capital scales with *ongoing* participation and is capital-efficient for honest users (they get it back). A one-time sunk cost "is incurred once and does not scale with ongoing participation" — amortizable across time [arXiv:2601.03923, `[SOURCED]`].
- STAKESURE reports that locked stake becomes *genuinely* at-risk only with **attributable slashing plus redistribution** — "transforms staking from merely locked capital into genuinely at-risk capital" [arXiv:2401.05797]. PACT's S4 slash is attributable (content-addressed `target_stake_id`, `slash.js:15-32`) and quorum-gated (`stake-anchor.js:71-84`).

**The case against at-risk / for sunk cost — and the counter-attacks the literature surfaced:**

- **At-risk capital defeated by capital-efficiency**: "an attacker willing to immobilize funds across many accounts faces only *opportunity costs* ... rather than permanent losses. This makes capital locking weaker than capital burning" [FOB21 r3]. Absent *detection*, a slashable stake is merely locked, not lost.
- **Liquid staking / restaking (LSTs/LRTs)** let the same staked capital secure multiple protocols and stay tradable, collapsing effective opportunity cost [threesigma.xyz LST/LRT].
- **Capital pooling**: "if multiple accounts share access to the same deposit or collateral pool, the per-account cost drops dramatically" [FOB21 r3].
- **Stake-grinding / wallet-splitting**: under any concave rule an attacker splits evenly and scales `n` to recover linear power [arXiv:2605.18990, Lemma 5.1, `[SOURCED]`].
- **Griefing**: an attacker can burn its own at-risk stake to impose disproportionate cost on honest parties — deterred **only by a non-zero burn floor** exceeding attack benefit [arXiv:2005.09327; STAKESURE].

**Doctrine verdict.** The strongest design *combines* at-risk stake (for attributable faults) with a **burn floor** (unconditional, for griefing) — neither alone dominates. At-risk dominates on capital-efficiency-for-honest-users and on being ongoing/scalable; sunk cost dominates on *unconditional* per-attempt deterrence.

> ⚠️ **Over-claim guard — this is where PACT's stake arc is honestly weakest.** PACT's S1-S5 stake carries **no amount and no `anchor_ref`** (`stake.js:3-10`, D5: a self-asserted `{stake:1e9}` body scores identically and is forgeable). So the in-memory stake is **neither at-risk nor sunk — it is a bare presence/lock marker.** It occupies the "at-risk" *slot* in the ladder but bears *no cost of either kind* until S6 chain-settlement is deployed. The §2 table cell for rung 3 says so directly (not only here). Reporting rung 3 as delivering at-risk deterrence today is a category error the code's own comment pre-empts (`issuance-policy.js:56`: "PRESENCE, not forfeitable cost -- SHADOW").

> ⚠️ **Over-claim guard — the slash path is not symmetric.** The reviewed draft claimed "slander is exactly as costly as support." That is overstated. Verified against source: the crater quorum needs ≥2 DISTINCT earned-standing human roots (`stake-anchor.js:71-84`), and earned standing = "authored ≥1 CLAIM" (`standing.js:30-37`) — a **one-time, sunk, non-forfeitable** entry price, re-keyed to `rootOf`. So `rootOf` collapses *persona-multiplied* slander to one root, but a **capital-pooled multi-root adversary** (the exact U1 threat) craters any honest staker's stake at only the sunk cost of N trivial CLAIM records, **with no forfeiture on the accuser**. The slash path therefore carries the §3 griefing hole and needs a **burn floor on the SLASHER**, not only on the slashee (see §8, Q5). The precise claim: persona-multiplied slander collapses to root; multi-root pooled slander is a cheap DoS on honest stakers once the crater gates.

## 4. Payoff-relative sizing

The cost floor need not be absolute — it need only exceed what a fake root can **extract**. Formally this is the cost-of-corruption vs profit-from-corruption frame: a system is secure when `CoC >> PfC` [chain.link/article/cryptoeconomic-security; arXiv:2401.05797 — external, unverified here].

The load-bearing PACT-specific consequence: **PACT weights are SHADOW and gate nothing today (`convert.actionable === false`, INV-16, `convert.js:149`), so the Sybil payoff `PfC ≈ 0`.** A fake root can extract nothing, because no weight it inflates gates an irreversible action (NS-8). Therefore the required cost floor is ~0 today, and **rises only as (and only if) a root gains gating power.**

**This is a SEQUENCING heuristic, not a safety property.** The reviewed draft claimed the floor and payoff "must be armed together ... in the same step" and treated that as a guarantee. It is not — the note's own text admits two *separately* armable actions ("arming the floor ahead of the payoff is wasted; arming the payoff ahead of the floor is the NS-8 violation"), which is exactly the proof they *can* desynchronize. Two consequences the doctrine must name rather than assert away:

- **No coupling mechanism exists today.** The only cited guarantee is `convert.js:98-124` returning `funded_root:null` fail-closed — but that guards only the case where the anchor is **absent/broken/throwing**, not the case where a stake axis is **present-but-costless** while a weight starts gating. A future consumer that flips `actionable=true` reading a `status:'locked'` *free presence lock* as "funded" satisfies every present code check (`meetsPolicy` returns true for a locked zero-amount stake, `issuance-policy.js:44-46`) yet the payoff is now positive and the floor is still zero.
- **PfC is unbounded and correlated for a governance weight**, not a per-attempt bounded extraction. §6 cites Buterin's "~33 bits deanonymize by behavior" and correlated-governance-capture: a captured trust weight's payoff is open-ended across many gated actions. "Size the floor to the payoff" is therefore **not computable in the general case** — the floor must be super-linear (personhood) or the weight must stay ungated, not "sized."

**Proposed invariant (for whenever a weight would gate):** `actionable` MUST NOT flip true while `stakeOf` can return `locked` for a zero-amount stake — i.e. a future gater must key on a **non-null `anchor_ref`/amount**, never on `status` alone. This is not built (rung 3 is SHADOW); it is the precondition that closes the desync hole before any gating.

> ⚠️ **Over-claim guard.** Payoff-relative sizing justifies why the arc can be SHADOW now (`PfC ≈ 0`); it does **not** license flipping `actionable` true on the theory that "the payoff is still small." Arming the floor *ahead* of the payoff is wasted SHADOW machinery (the §6/§7 drift); arming the payoff *ahead* of the floor — or on a costless presence lock — is the NS-8 violation. The `funded_root: null` fail-closed contract (`convert.js:98-124`) covers only the *absent-axis* case; the *present-but-costless* case needs the invariant above.

## 5. The privacy ↔ dedup tension (verified against the attestation research)

The doctrine spine claimed on-device biometric is privacy-preserving *precisely because* it withholds a cross-account human token, so it can harden a *different* axis (custody/liveness) but cannot anchor *uniqueness*. **The attestation literature confirms this in strong, specific form** — it is a designed property of mainstream platform attestation, not an accident. (All bullets below are external citations, unverified from within this note — re-probe before relying.)

- **WebAuthn/FIDO batch attestation and Anonymization CAs are *engineered* to prevent per-device dedup.** FIDO recommends ≥100,000-device batches sharing one attestation key so a relying party "cannot determine whether the authenticator represents device A or device B" [fidoalliance.org 2024 white paper]. An Anonymization CA "dynamically generates per-credential attestation certificates such that the attestation statements ... do not provide uniquely identifiable information" [W3C WebAuthn L2].
- **Apple Anonymous Attestation** uses a **fixed AAGUID for all Apple devices** + a fresh per-credential cert "so that websites cannot look for the same certificate to track users" [Apple Passkey Attestation docs]. It proves "a real Apple device," never "this device."
- **Consumer synced passkeys send *no* attestation** (zeroed AAGUID) because a synced credential is not bound to any single device [corbado.com passkey-attestation].
- **Private Access Tokens (Privacy Pass, RFC 9578)** use blinded signatures so "device data is isolated and explicitly NOT exchanged"; the origin "cannot link the client's behavior" [educatedguesswork.org; blog.cloudflare.com]. `token_key_id` is truncated *specifically* "so Issuers cannot use it to uniquely identify Clients" [RFC 9578].
- **The narrow exceptions prove the rule.** The *only* mainstream schemes that permit private dedup are deliberately abuse-scoped and identifier-withholding: Play Integrity **device recall** (3 developer-defined bits, Google-mediated, "cannot fingerprint or track") and DeviceCheck (2 bits per developer) [developer.android.com/google/play/integrity/device-recall]. Full de-anonymized identification (Android ID attestation, enterprise attestation) is walled off to privileged/MDM callers [source.android.com keystore/attestation].

**The un-dedup-ability cuts both ways.** Because the fixed-AAGUID / anonymization-CA design *deliberately* prevents per-device dedup, it is not neutral — it is a **Sybil-laundering surface for the opposite goal**: an attacker with M devices (or M synced-passkey contexts, which send zeroed attestation) is **indistinguishable from M humans** to a relying party. Privacy-preserving-against-tracking and un-dedup-able-toward-uniqueness are the *same* property read from two directions. This concretely strengthens the "never score rung 2 as U1" rule: it is not merely that device attestation *fails to prove* uniqueness — it *actively laundabout* a device farm into apparent multi-human presence if any consumer ever mistakes rung 2 for a uniqueness signal.

**The synthesis is a genuine fundamental tension** [attestation finding 12]: you *cannot* get open, cross-app, privacy-preserving unique-human dedup from platform attestation alone — "that gap is exactly what the personhood-credential / World-ID line of work targets." Personhood oracles obtain a uniqueness token precisely by **not** being privacy-preserving *in the dedup sense*: World ID dedups against the global IrisCode set [en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_(blockchain)]; PoH publishes face+video on-chain [docs.kleros.io]. World ID then *re-adds* privacy at the *presentation* layer via ZK nullifiers — but the uniqueness itself is born at a **non-private global enrollment** step, never from the device.

### The custody-vs-uniqueness split (the NS-9 line)

This is the doctrine's sharpest distinction, and it maps directly onto PACT's live custody arc:

- A **biometric-gated Secure-Enclave key** is a real HARDEN of the **custody** axis. Secure Enclave keys are P-256-only, non-exportable, device-bound, not synced, with usage gated by Face ID/Touch ID via a `SecAccessControl` policy [support.apple.com secure-enclave]. This is a genuine world-anchored custody boundary — it would **retire the `merlin95` `0600`-file ceiling** (root-key custody as same-uid file perms; `sigma-root-deploy.md` "integrity, not a hardened HSM boundary — deferred") by moving the private key behind hardware + biometric presence.
- **But it is not a U1 signal.** The device-binding is a property of *the key*, not something a relying party can turn into a stable *human* identity (that requires attestation, which for consumer passkeys is withheld — above). It hardens *who-holds-the-key* and *is-a-live-human-present*, never *is-this-a-distinct-human*.

**Keep them on separate axes and separate ledgers.** Custody hardening (rung 2 / Secure Enclave) advances the provenance/custody frontier (north-star §4, custody bullet); it must **never** be scored as an advance on U1. Collapsing them is exactly the NS-2 violation (integrity ≠ provenance ≠ validity) and the north-star §6 drift ("authenticity treated as independence").

> ⚠️ **Over-claim guard.** A future PACT wave that wires a biometric Secure-Enclave key MUST report it in the custody column, not the U1 column, and MUST NOT let `convert` read it as an epistemic (axis-4) or uniqueness signal. The `funded_root` axis-1 discipline (`convert.js:96-98`, "NEVER read as epistemic independence axis 4") is the pattern to replicate.

## 6. Latest advancements (2025-26) — grounded survey

**Provenance note (NS-9):** this entire section is an **external survey; every figure below is unverified from within this note as of 2026-07-05 — re-probe before relying.** The specific point-estimates (user counts, dollar figures, dates, author counts) carry the classic overclaim shape and should be read as *reported, not confirmed*. What is load-bearing for the doctrine is the section's *conclusion* (the over-claim guard at the end), which rests on the north-star's own §4/§5 reasoning, not on any of these numbers.

The field splits into three families with no clean winner; the design trilemma is **uniqueness-strength vs. privacy vs. inclusivity/scale** [digitap.app; defi-planet.com 2025/10].

**Biometric hard-uniqueness (highest assurance, heaviest external risk).** World (rebranded from Worldcoin, ~Oct 2024) derives an IrisCode via the Orb and dedups against prior codes [en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_(blockchain)]. Its 2024-25 privacy upgrade reportedly runs iris comparison under Anonymized MPC — codes stay encrypted, only a binary match/no-match is revealed, across independent nodes [world.org SMPC blog; arXiv:2405.04463]. **But**: reported bans/deletion orders across multiple jurisdictions (Brazil, Indonesia, Philippines, Thailand, Kenya, Hong Kong, Spain) and a reported China MSS national-security warning [coindesk.com 2025/08; restofworld.org/2026]. Crucially, its real attack surface is the **credential/enrollment layer, not the iris capture**: a reported black market resells verified World IDs (~$30), operator credentials were reportedly stolen, and a bug reportedly let anyone become an operator [gizmodo.com; finance.yahoo.com]. So the nullifier guarantees **one-credential-per-enrolled-iris, not one-per-human**: a device-farm / paid-enrollee / coerced-enrollee / rented-iris pipeline mints many credentials to one operator, and the resale market makes the credential itself money-purchasable — **money-buys-roots relocates from the identity layer to the enrollment/resale layer, it does not vanish.** Honest gap: I found **no verified public disclosure** of a successful spoof of the Orb's live iris capture itself [confidence: medium].

**Social / behavioral (privacy-friendlier, weaker Sybil resistance, hard to scale).** BrightID (social-graph connection parties, no biometrics — bootstraps slowly, collusion-vulnerable) [web3classdao.github.io]; Idena (synchronous "flip" reverse-Turing ceremonies — reported ~7,000-8,600 validating identities, LLM/vision progress threatens flip AI-resistance) [docs.idena.io]; Proof of Humanity (vouched public registry + Kleros arbitration — reported ~19,000 registered, weak privacy by design) [docs.kleros.io].

**Aggregation / ZK-plumbing (most deployed, probabilistic).** Human Passport (ex-Gitcoin Passport, reportedly acquired by Holonym Foundation Feb 2025) aggregates "Stamps" into a probabilistic Humanity Score, reportedly protecting airdrops across many partners — but "raises Sybil cost rather than making it impossible" [human.tech; coindesk.com 2025/02]. Privado ID (ex-Polygon ID, reportedly spun out June 2024) supplies the ZK-credential *rail*; uniqueness must come from whatever issuer mints the credential — **it is plumbing, not a personhood guarantee** [theblock.co].

**The dominant 2025-26 intellectual shift** is Buterin's ~June 2025 argument against strict one-per-person ZK IDs: "ZK on its own does not protect you from coercion," ~33 bits deanonymize by behavior correlation, so ZK-encrypting the identifier does not prevent behavioral deanonymization; the safer design is **pluralistic identity** — "no single dominant issuing authority," realized via social-graph systems (Circles) or many ID providers so none nears 100% share [biometricupdate.com 2025/06; cryptoslate.com]. The critique implicitly targets the one-Orb-per-human model.

**The frontier response to the amortization result** (per the same `[SOURCED]` preprint): because a one-time personhood cost is amortizable, the leading edge moves toward **continuous / rate-limited** human verification. The Human Challenge Oracle binds each active identity to fresh, non-parallelizable human cognition *every time window* — "identities must continuously pay rent," recovering linear *and ongoing* Sybil cost [arXiv:2601.03923, `[SOURCED]`]. And a 2024 Personhood Credentials paper (reported large multi-institution authorship) formalizes per-human, nullifier-bounded, ZK-presented credentials where uniqueness comes from the **enrollment** step, never the device [arxiv.org/html/2408.07892v1; technologyreview.com 2024/09].

> ⚠️ **Over-claim guard for PACT.** None of these is a drop-in that would let PACT *close* U1. Each relocates the anchor to an external oracle with its own live regulatory, coercion, and credential-market attack surface — and, per the World example, re-admits money at the enrollment/resale layer. Adopting one is a decision to **inherit that oracle's trust anchor wholesale** — an NS-7 world-anchored dependency, not an in-process win. Buterin's pluralistic direction is the honest fit for PACT's "registry-not-oracle" stance (§7), but pluralism *dilutes* the uniqueness guarantee by design — it is a coercion-safety trade, not a stronger U1.

## 7. Mapping onto PACT

**The stake arc (S1-S6) is the ladder's rung 3, built SHADOW, and it narrows — it does not harden.**

- **S1 `StakeAnchor` / `stakeOf`** (`stake-anchor.js`): a derived-on-read TRUST-layer view (like `direct.js`/`consensus.js`), never a store/oracle/gate. Reads through the one authenticated-minter gate (`verifiedRecords`, INV-14): a stake counts **only if its signature verifies under the sender's registered key**; unsigned/bad-sig/unregistered contributes 0 — store-presence is never provenance (#273 family, NS-2). Keyed by `rootOf(src_persona_did)` so a forged `parent_human_uid` is structurally ignored. Lives in `trust/` not `identity/` to avoid a reverse layering edge.
